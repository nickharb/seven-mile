A Brief Argument on Behalf of the Census

This video was created for the Center for American Progress. It explains why the U.S. Census is so important to your daily life from being able to get to work or buying a new couch for your apartment. As is turns out, the Census is a lot cooler than we thought.

Where Every Pregnancy is a Gamble

Photographer Ami Vitale and I produced this together for the Knight Center for International Media. After a decade-long conflict, Sierra Leone has many challenges ahead including improving child and maternal health. In 2009, one in eight women died during pregnancy. Fatimata Konte, an expecting mother, fears giving birth after already losing five of her children. She hopes the new policy to bring free health care to all pregnant women will save her next child and make giving birth safe for all women.

Dirty Water, Dirty Money

This video was created for Center for American Progress and follows the story of one North Carolina family's fight against Duke Energy to prevent toxic chemicals from coal ash from contaminating their drinking water. The video focuses on the recent repeal of the state's judicial public financing system, giving big companies more influence over the court system by allowing private contributions in judicial elections.

Ocean Acidification: An East Coast Problem Worth Watching

This video was created for Ocean Conservancy about the emerging threat of Ocean Acidification on the East Coast. While states like California on the West Coast have been tackling this problem for years, passing new legislation and bringing in science experts to study and work on the problem, the East Coast is only now beginning to see the effects of this issue and have little knowledge or support on how to prepare themselves.

The Last Word

In the 1970s, a Cambodian father perished at the hands of the Khmer Rouge, leaving only a diary. Over the course of this project, I became intimately familiar with his life, studying his diary and digging through family photos to tell his story. I worked closely with my colleague and friend to craft the accompanying video piece, as well as a translator and photographer, on what became one of my most personally meaningful projects.

The Goal of Life

This was one of the largest undertakings of my career to date. The Goal of Life was an interactive reporting package for the Associated Press. I took the creative lead on the project, providing editorial and artistic direction to a team of five, including two reporters, a photographer, videographer and producer. I also served as the lead designer and developer.

The Workers

The New York Times produced some incredible coverage for the 10th anniversary of the 9/11 attacks. Working with staff photographer Fred Conrad, I designed and developed The Workers, an interactive feature for NYT. In the weeks leading up to the anniversary I conducted interviews with many of the men and women rebuilding the towers, combining their voices with Conrad’s striking photography.

Voting Trends

Being in an international newsroom during the 2012 presidential election is an experience I’ll never forget. Voting Trends was an interactive data visualization I created for Associated Press as part of their 2012 election coverage. It explores presidential election history in the U.S. and voting patterns among various demographics.
